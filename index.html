Small ceremonies, Jan 29

I make a cup of coffee,
brush my teeth, tidy my bed.
I go in my head over the duties
to take on my way.

Small ceremonies
that turn the day unique to me.
Nothing heroic yet.
Just the willingness to begin.

Build, maintain, polish, work, invent
I tend to what is mine to tend:
my body, my mind, I learn, explore, create
I care with full intent.

Read a few pages, lift a weight right,
leave a streak of color, capture the light,
From the outside it might seem like labour,
but I know better, these are open doors and I do myself a favour.

Each moment teaches me something quiet.
Not all at once, or with spectacle,
but in tiny little fragments.
A steadier breath, a calmer demeanor,
a kinder heart to others and myself,
a hand less afraid to try, oh so eager.

I embrace myself again,
not through grand reinvention
its a compromise that i've made to myself
the promise of daily effort
